Mechanistic Convergence of Psychedelic Plasticity and Pancreatic Malignancy

Preprint — AfricArXiv In Submission · 2026 Caroline Wambui Gachema

Abstract

Psilocybin-assisted therapy is entering oncology settings at scale: clinical trials now enrol patients with cancer-related psychological distress, and advancing Phase 3 data for treatment-resistant depression have accelerated its broader integration into cancer care. Its therapeutic mechanism has been substantially reframed by Moliner, who demonstrated that psilocin (the active metabolite of psilocybin) directly binds the TrkB receptor as a positive allosteric modulator, positioning neurotrophin signalling as the primary driver of psychedelic-induced neuroplasticity.

Concurrently, pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDAC), a malignancy with near-universal perineural invasion (PNI) and a 12-month median survival post-diagnosis, is driven at the neural interface by nerve growth factor (NGF) signalling through TrkA, TrkB’s closest structural paralogue, sharing approximately 60% intracellular kinase domain identity and activating identical downstream effectors: MAPK/ERK, PI3K/Akt, and PLCγ. Critically, TrkB is also overexpressed in PDAC tumour cells, where it promotes epithelial-mesenchymal transition and anoikis resistance.

This paper maps the structural and signalling convergence of TrkA and TrkB; reviews the emergence of cancer neuroscience as a field; traces psilocybin’s clinical trajectory in oncology populations; and proposes organoid-based neuro-tumour chip models and generative AI-driven pathway simulation as the minimum preclinical infrastructure required to characterise this interface before it is encountered in clinical practice.

Computational Validation: TCGA-PAAD Analysis

To provide empirical grounding for the molecular hypothesis, I analysed NTRK1 (TrkA) and NTRK2 (TrkB) expression across the TCGA-PAAD cohort (n = 182 pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma tumour samples).

Expression of NTRK1 and NTRK2 in PDAC

Both neurotrophin receptors are expressed across the full PDAC cohort, with NTRK1 showing a higher median expression (0.95 log₂ units) relative to NTRK2 (0.32 log₂ units). The right-skewed distribution of NTRK1 indicates a subset of tumours with very high TrkA expression, consistent with its established role in driving perineural invasion.

Co-expression of NTRK1 and NTRK2

A significant positive correlation exists between NTRK1 and NTRK2 expression across PDAC tumors (Spearman ρ = 0.416, p < 0.001). This co-expression pattern establishes that both receptors are simultaneously active within the same tumor microenvironmental context - a prerequisite for the cross-talk the paper proposes.

Survival Analysis

Kaplan-Meier analysis did not demonstrate a statistically significant association between NTRK2 expression and overall survival at median-split dichotomisation (HR = 0.89, 95% CI 0.59–1.33, log-rank p = 0.57), nor for NTRK1 (HR = 0.75, 95% CI 0.50–1.13, p = 0.17). This null finding is consistent with the uniformly poor prognosis of PDAC across molecular subtypes and the limited statistical power of n = 182 for detecting moderate gene-level effects. The paper’s central argument is mechanistic rather than prognostic.

Pathway Enrichment

GSEA-KEGG analysis, with genes ranked by Spearman correlation with NTRK2 expression across all TCGA-PAAD samples, identified MAPK signalling and PI3K-Akt signalling as significantly positively enriched pathways in the NTRK2 co-expression network.

These pathways are precisely the downstream effectors shared between TrkA (perineural invasion) and TrkB (psilocin target), providing direct empirical support for the molecular convergence hypothesis.
